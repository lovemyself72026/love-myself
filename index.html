What is Calisthenics?
Calisthenics means using your own bodyweight as resistance no gym, no equipment. Push-ups, squats, planks that's calisthenics. It builds real strength, flexibility, and body control at your own pace.
What does "3 sets × 10" mean?
Sets = how many times you repeat a group of reps. Reps = how many times you do one movement. So 3 sets × 10 reps means: do the exercise 10 times, rest, do it again, rest, do it one more time. That's it 🤍

A Simple Method
1
Pray before you open it
Ask the Holy Spirit to be your teacher. The Bible is spiritually discerned: ask God to open your eyes before you read a single word. (Psalm 119:18, John 16:13)
2
Observe: What does it say?
Read slowly. Read it more than once. Note who is speaking, who it's written to, what is happening. Don't rush past the words: let them land.
3
Interpret: What does it mean?
What was God saying to the original audience? Context matters: a verse never means something it couldn't have meant when it was written. Look at the surrounding passages.
4
Apply: What does it mean for me?
How does this change the way you think, speak, or act today? The goal of Bible study is not information: it is transformation. (James 1:22, Romans 12:2)
5
Meditate: Let it stay with you
Carry a verse through your day. Mull it over. Come back to it. Biblical meditation is not emptying your mind: it is filling it with God's Word until it changes how you see everything. (Joshua 1:8, Psalm 1:2–3)
6
Let Scripture interpret Scripture
When you don't understand a passage, look for other places in the Bible that speak to the same theme. The Bible is its own best commentary: it is one unified story pointing to Jesus. (Luke 24:27)

His Daily Rhythm
Early Morning
He rose before dawn to pray alone
Before the crowds came, before the demands of the day, Jesus was already with the Father. He withdrew to isolated places: a mountain, a garden, the wilderness: and spent the early hours in prayer. This was not occasional. It was His rhythm. (Mark 1:35, Luke 6:12)
Morning
He taught, healed, and engaged with people
Jesus was deeply present with people. He taught in synagogues and on hillsides, healed those who came to Him, and engaged with everyone: the religious, the outcast, the children, the desperate. He was never in a hurry, yet He was always purposeful. (Luke 4:16, Matthew 9:35)
Midday
He shared meals and walked with His disciples
Much of Jesus' ministry happened around a table or on the road. He ate with tax collectors and sinners, fed thousands, and used everyday moments: a meal, a journey, a well: to speak life into people. He was fully human: He got hungry, tired, and sat down to rest. (Luke 19:5, John 4:6, John 6:11)
Evening
He gathered with His disciples and withdrew to rest
Evenings were often for deeper conversation with the twelve: unpacking what had happened, answering their questions, preparing their hearts. After ministry He would withdraw, sometimes crossing the lake just to have space. He honoured rest and did not run on empty. (Mark 4:35, John 13–17)
Always
He only did what He saw the Father doing
The thread through every part of His day was this: total surrender to the Father. He did not act on His own initiative. He listened, He obeyed, He moved in step with the Spirit. His whole life: morning to night: was one continuous act of dependence and love. (John 5:19, John 8:28)
